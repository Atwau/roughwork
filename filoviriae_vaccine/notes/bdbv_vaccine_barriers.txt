# Primary Barriers to BDBV Vaccine Development

## Species-Specific Protection Limits: 
#* Licensed vaccines currently exist for Zaire ebolavirus, and active development is underway
for Sudan virus and Marburg virus candidates. However, scientific evidence indicates that protection
is highly species-specific. Any potential cross-protection offered by these existing vaccines against
BDBV remains highly uncertain and is insufficiently demonstrated to rely upon for outbreak control.  

## Absence of a Commercial Market:
#* Bundibugyo ebolavirus disease is geographically confined to Africa. Because there is no established
commercial market for vaccines against this specific virus, vaccine developers face immense economic 
uncertainty, which historically stalls long-term discovery and production pipelines.

## Lag in Clinical Advancement:
#* Despite broader advancements in filovirus countermeasures over the last decade, BDBV research has
significantly lagged behind. Prior to recent accelerated funding calls, no BDBV-specific vaccine
candidate had ever advanced into Phase 1 clinical evaluation, leaving a severe gap in critical preclinical,
translational, and manufacturing data.
